Coding Hackathon Rules and Regulations

  1. Eligibility: Participants must meet the eligibility criteria specified for the hackathon, including age, skill level, and any other relevant requirements.
  2. Team Size: Teams should consist of the maximum number of members allowed, as specified in the hackathon guidelines. No exceptions will be made.
  3. Registration: All participants must register in advance through the official registration process. Late registrations may not be accepted.
  4. Code of Conduct: Respectful and inclusive behavior is expected from all participants. Harassment, discrimination, or any form of misconduct will not be tolerated.
  5. Original Work: Participants must submit original work developed during the hackathon. Plagiarism or using pre-existing code without disclosure is prohibited.
  6. Submission Deadline: Project submissions must be made within the specified deadline. Late submissions will not be considered for judging.
  7. Project Scope: Projects must align with the specified theme or problem statement of the hackathon. Deviating from the theme may result in disqualification.
  8. Demo Requirement: All teams must present a live demo or a video demonstration of their project during the judging phase. Failure to do so may impact judging.
  9. Judging Criteria: Projects will be evaluated based on predefined judging criteria, including innovation, functionality, and presentation. Judges' decisions are final.
  10. Ownership and Licensing: Participants retain ownership of their projects unless otherwise specified. Clarify the licensing and usage rights of submitted projects.
  11. Tools and Technologies: Participants must adhere to the list of allowed tools, programming languages, and technologies as specified in the hackathon guidelines.
  12. Intellectual Property: Specify the handling of intellectual property. Decide whether projects will remain open-source or if there are any restrictions.
  13. Communication: Participants can use designated communication channels for questions and clarifications. Organizers will provide timely responses.
  14. Prizes and Awards: Prizes and awards will be granted to winners and possibly to projects in special categories (e.g., best design, best innovation). Details will be provided.
  15. Disqualification: Teams or participants may be disqualified for violating rules, cheating, or engaging in inappropriate behavior. The decision of the organizers is final.
